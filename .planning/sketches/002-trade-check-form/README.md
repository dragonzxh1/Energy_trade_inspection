# Sketch 002: Trade Check Form

## Design Question
Does the Variant D control quality translate to the real Trade Check form? And which layout structure serves the workflow best?

## How to View
```
open .planning/sketches/002-trade-check-form/index.html
```

Toolbar (bottom-right) includes theme switcher: **Dark** (default) and **Light**.

## Variants
- **A: Single Column** — Form top, results scroll into view below on submit. Simple, focused. Works at any width.
- **B: Split Panel** — Form pinned left (380px), results always visible right. Recent checks in sidebar. Suits power users who run many checks.
- **C: Stepper / Wizard** — Three-step flow: Parties → Trade Details → Review & Run. Progressive disclosure. Reduces perceived complexity.

## What to Look For
- Does the split panel (B) feel cramped on the form side, or just right?
- Does the stepper (C) feel bureaucratic, or appropriately guided?
- How does the score card read in light vs dark theme?
- Is the "Recent Checks" list in B useful or distracting?
